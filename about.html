Who I Am

Wangui Gatere-Brenda is an Administrative Specialist and community-focused leader with a deep passion for education, youth mentorship, and inclusive development in Nyeri County and Kenya's mountain regions.

Born and raised in Nyeri, I have witnessed firsthand the challenges and opportunities facing our communities, particularly women and marginalized groups in rural and mountain areas.

My Values

  • Integrity: Upholding honesty and ethical standards in all endeavors
  • Service: Putting community needs before personal interests
  • Inclusivity: Ensuring all voices are heard, especially marginalized communities
  • Accountability: Taking responsibility for actions and decisions
  • Empowerment: Equipping women and youth with tools for self-reliance

My Journey

2015

USIU University Graduate

Graduated from United States International University (USIU) with a degree that equipped me with strong organizational, leadership, and administrative skills essential for effective public service.

2016-2020

Administrative Specialist Career

Developed professional expertise in coordination, planning, and people-centered service across various organizations, focusing on efficiency and community impact.

2021-Present

Community Advocacy

Actively engaged in community development initiatives across Nyeri County, with particular focus on women's empowerment, youth mentorship, and education in mountain regions.

2023

Declaration of Candidacy

Announced intention to run for Nyeri Women Representative in the 2027 elections, focusing on issues affecting women in mountain regions and marginalized communities.

Why Public Service

My decision to enter public service is deeply personal and rooted in my experiences growing up in Nyeri. I have seen the resilience of our women, the potential of our youth, and the challenges facing marginalized communities in our mountain regions.

Public service, to me, is about responsibility. It is about showing up, listening carefully, and working with people to find practical solutions. I believe leadership should uplift, not divide, and should always be grounded in integrity.

"I believe Nyeri deserves leadership that listens, learns, and delivers. Together, we can build a county where every woman is empowered and every child has a future."

My professional background in administration has taught me the importance of organization, planning, and accountability—skills I believe are essential for effective representation and service delivery.
